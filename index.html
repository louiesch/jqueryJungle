Gorillas are ground-dwelling, predominantly herbivorous apes that inhabit the forest of central Sub-Saharan Africa. They are the largest living primates.

Sloths are a group of arboreal Neotropical xenathran mammals, constituting the suborder Folivora. Noted for their slowness of movement, they spend most of their lives hanging upside down in the trees of the tropical rainforests of South America and Central America.

The lion (Panthera leo) is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera. It is sexually dimorphic; adult male lions have a prominent mane. Lions are a social species, forming groups called prides.
